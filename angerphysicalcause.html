But Your Honor, I Had My Period!

Addressing Physical Causes of Anger

Yes, women have used having their period as a defense against committing murder. I've listed some other factors that can cause anger issues that I've experienced and witnessed in people I care about.

Let's Start With ... A Woman's Cycle

For women, issues with her menstrual cycle and menopause and related hormones. Record your moods for a month or two to notice patterns.

Liver Issues

Issues with the liver, such as Hepatitis C, and cirrhosis of the liver

Insomnia

Anger can add to insomnia, and lack of sleep can increase anger.

Cortisol and Caffeine

Anything affecting cortisol levels including caffeine and other stimulants

Physical Pain and Inflammation

Physical issues that cause chronic or acute pain and inflammation

Medication Side Effects

Side effects of psychiatric or other prescription medication

Withdrawals

Withdrawal from addictive substances, behaviors and withdrawal from prescription medications

Brain Trauma

Acute or repeated brain trauma from fights or car accidents

Intense Hunger/ Blood Sugar Issues

Starving yourself and not getting enough to eat on a regular basis can increase anger

In conclusion if you are having issues with anger it's good to go through the below list and make sure none of the following things are adding to the problem.

  • A) Be aware of menstrual cycle or other changes in a woman’s body
  • B) Get treatment for serious issues of the liver
  • C) See the section on getting good sleep
  • D) If caffeine or other stimulants are increasing your anger moderate or cut out completely
  • E) Get treatment for acute and chronic pain and inflammation issues
  • F) Cut down or eliminate medications that are causing anger issues
  • G) Be aware that withdrawal symptoms don’t last forever
  • H) Get treatment for brain trauma
  • I) Eat enough and often enough if hunger is triggering too much anger
