Ending the Madness

How To Become More Peaceful

It's been important for my sanity to create a life that revolves around not challenging my ability to stay calm.

Long Term Strategies to Reduce Levels of Anger

Once you understand what triggers your anger and fear you can start making long term changes to make sure you don’t end up in triggering situations.

Put up boundaries with people - The quicker you can put up a boundary with someone when you are experiencing mild levels of anger the better. If the person then respects the boundary then that should solve the issue. Otherwise the anger grows and grows and the other person might not be aware they are making us angry if we aren’t communicating it.

Avoiding situations that we know will make us angry - For example if I know that I get easily angry in situations that are very noisy, I shouldn’t apply to a job in a bowling alley or a nightclub with loud music. Once you pinpoint the thing that triggers you, it’s okay to make your life easier by trying to avoid having to be around that trigger on a regular basis.

Having a plan if we know that we are going into a situation that will trigger us - If you know you are going to have to enter a triggering situation make sure you plan ahead. Meditate or do a slow breathing exercise, get enough sleep the night before, talk to a supportive friend, listen to music you like, and then go into the situation and put up boundaries.

This should decrease your shame and fear over time as you are able to spot and handle these overreactions in increasingly constructive ways and gain control over your life.
